Adding Design Elements:

Adding and removing text boxes

Text is the most basic element you can add to your project, and it's also the most flexible.

 

To add a text box to your project:

  1. Click the Text tool button in the Edit toolbar.

  2. Use the mouse to position the cursor arrow in the area where you want to add the text.

  3. Click and hold down the left mouse button.

  4. Drag the mouse down and to the right to create a rectangular box.

  5. Release the mouse button.

    The text box appears with a blinking cursor to show its ready for you to begin typing.

To position a text box:

  1. Place the cursor arrow over the text box and click the left mouse button to select it.

    The small boxes appear around the text to show that it’s selected.

  1. Click anywhere on the edge of the box and hold down the mouse button.

Notice that the arrow cursor changed to a multi-arrow “move” cursor.

  1. Move the text box to the desired location, then let go of the mouse button.

If another element is in front of the box, you may have to send it behind the picture using the Send to Back command in the Arrange / Layer menu.

To remove a text box from your project:

  1. Click anywhere on the text box to select it

    (If another element is in front of the box, you may have to send it behind the text box using the Send to Back command in the Arrange / Layer menu.)

  1. Press the Delete key, or click on the Delete command in the Edit menu.
