Adding Design Elements:

Text-Changing text box size and shape

Think of text boxes as miniature word-processor windows. When you create a text box or when you click on it, small dots appear around the text, creating the “box”.

The dots define the boundaries of the text box, but they are also “handles” that you can click on to change the size and shape of the text box. Changing the size and shape of the text box causes the text inside to change.

Changing the Size of Text Boxes:

 

By clicking on a handle, holding down the mouse button, and dragging the handle around, you can quickly re-size a text box in the manner you see here.

Rotating Text Boxes:
The handle that sticks out from the rest is the rotation handle (highlighted in red below). Click and drag this handle around to rotate the text, or click the right side of the status bar to rotate a text box.

Rotation Tip: Hold down the 'Shift' key while dragging to restrict the rotation to increments of 45°.
