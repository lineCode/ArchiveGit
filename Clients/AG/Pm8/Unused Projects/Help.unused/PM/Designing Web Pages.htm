Designing Web Pages

PrintMaster needs to convert your web page projects to Hypertext Markup Language (HTML) - the special document format required by web browsers - before you can view them on the Internet. Since some of the layout and formatting options available in normal PrintMaster projects are not supported in HTML, when you design Web pages there are some special considerations to keep in mind...

Overlapping Objects:
When two objects overlap on a web page project, PrintMaster will turn both objects into one graphic object during the HTML-conversion process. Readability will be retained, but the addition of the graphic to your web page may increase its download time considerably.

When two objects overlap on a web page project, PrintMaster will alert you that the objects will be converted to a graphic by a flashing border when they intersect.

Formatted Text:
Not all text formatting will convert to HTML. For instance, text that has been stretched, rotated, or filled-in with a pattern in PrintMaster will be converted to a graphic during the HTML-conversion process.

Web Page Design Tips:
To keep your web pages running smoothly on the Internet:

  • Keep your graphics small.

  • Use standard fonts like Arial, Courier, Times, and Wingdings. If you use unusual fonts or formatting, they may not display properly on other computers.

  • Use text sizes between 10 and 30 points.

  • Single-space all text.

  • For best legibility, keep most of your text left-aligned.

  • Avoid tabs. They will be converted into spaces in HTML.

  • Don't underline text unless it's a hyperlink.

  • If you do add color to text, be sure it's readable against the web page's background and not easily confused with a hyperlink.

  • Use colors consistently to identify hyperlinks.

  • Avoid rotated, shadowed, and stretched text.
