DESIGN BASICS:

Adding Design Elements:
Elements: Images

PrintMaster displays your images and shapes on screen so you can easily tell what your page will look like when printed. When you select a image or shape to work on, PrintMaster places a frame around it to indicate its exact position and size.

To add an image to your project:

The Art Gallery contains thousands of drawings, photos, borders, and paintings, many created specially for PrintMaster. You can also select your own image.

  1. Click the Picture button (shown above) in the Edit toolbar.

  2. Use the Art Gallery to select a picture.

    After you make your selection, the picture appears in the Design Workspace screen, framed by small boxes.

Placing an image where you want:

  1. Place the cursor arrow over the image and click the left mouse button to select it.

    The small boxes appear around the image to show that it’s selected.

  2. Click anywhere on the image and hold down the mouse button.

    Notice that the arrow cursor changed to a multi-arrow “move” cursor.

  3. Move the image to the desired location, then let go of the mouse button.

    If another element is in front of the box, you may have to send it behind the image using the Send to Back command in the Arrange menu.

Changing the size of an image or shape:

If your image is too big or too small to fit properly in your project, the following steps will help you resize your image:

  1. Make sure your image is ‘active’ (handles showing around its edges). If it is not, click directly on your image to make it active. If you are having trouble selecting your image, hit the Tab key to cycle through the different levels of objects in your project until your image is active.

  2. Once your image is active, place the cursor on the handle (the small square box) on the bottom right corner of the image.

    • Drag the mouse diagonally out and down to make the image larger.

    • Drag the mouse up and to the left to make the image smaller.

    • To make sure the image keeps its exact proportions, hold down the Control (Ctrl) key while dragging.

Cropping an image:

Images may be too large or include things you’d like to remove. PrintMaster has a cropping tool that allows you to “cut out” a portion of the photo you want to see, without removing or disturbing any part of the image.

  1. Click the Crop tool on the Modify toolbar

    Make sure you first select the image you want (handles are showing around its edges).

  2. Place the cursor over any handle of the image.

    The cursor will turn into a cropping tool. You can now drag the handle to create a new window through which you will view your image.

Note: You are not altering the image, you are just reducing or enlarging the ‘window’ through which you can see that image.

  1. With your image still active and the cropping tool button selected, place the cursor over the center of the image.

    The cursor will turn into a ‘move’ hand. By clicking and dragging your image, you can move the image within the cropped window to see only the portion of the image you want.
